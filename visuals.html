Data & Visualizations

Visual insights into sea level rise trends

Data visualization translates complex climate observations into actionable insights. This section highlights observed trends, future projections, and indicators that inform coastal risk assessment and planning.

Key Indicators

Scientific measurements and climate models provide measurable indicators of sea level rise and its accelerating impacts.

🌍 Global Mean Sea Level Rise

Satellite altimetry observations show an average rise of approximately 3.4 mm per year, with acceleration observed in recent decades.

📈 Future Projections

Climate models project significant coastal inundation by 2100 under high-emission scenarios, increasing flood frequency and land loss.

Observed Trends

Rising sea levels interact with tides and storms, amplifying coastal hazards.

Increased frequency of coastal flooding during high tides
Accelerated shoreline erosion and loss of coastal ecosystems

Decision-Making Insights

Visual data supports evidence-based decision-making for long-term coastal adaptation strategies.

Identification of high-risk zones for infrastructure investment
Support for evacuation planning and emergency response strategies
