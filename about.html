Methodology & Assumptions

The content on this dashboard follows methodology commonly used in coastal planning. Since we use illustrative data, these describe how the content was derived‚Äînot how live data is processed:

Risk Zoning Framework

Coastal zones are classified by elevation above mean sea level and exposure to flooding. Low risk (>3m), medium risk (1‚Äì3m), and high risk (<1m) thresholds follow common practices in coastal planning literature. These are illustrative‚Äîactual zoning would use site-specific elevation data and local flood models.

Projection Scenarios

Sea level rise figures (e.g., ~30 cm by 2050) align with IPCC AR6 and NOAA mid-range scenarios. We use these as representative values. Actual outcomes depend on emissions pathways and regional factors such as land subsidence and ocean currents.

Impact Assessments

Infrastructure and settlement impacts are drawn from literature and case studies. Population and economic exposure figures (e.g., 200M+ at risk) are illustrative placeholders; real-world assessments require local census data, asset inventories, and engineering models.

Limitations

Data & Scope

  • Static data: Current dashboard uses representative values, not live API feeds or real-time measurements.
  • Global averages: Local conditions (subsidence, tides, storms) vary significantly and are not fully reflected.
  • Simplified models: Elevation-based zoning does not replace detailed hydraulic or flood modeling for specific sites.

Use Context

  • This dashboard is for educational and planning support, not regulatory or legal compliance.
  • Official flood maps and zoning decisions should rely on government-issued data and approved methodologies.

Future Scope

Planned Enhancements

  • API integration: Connect to NASA, NOAA, or similar APIs for real-time or updated sea level and coastal data.
  • Interactive maps: Add map-based visualization of risk zones and evacuation routes.
  • Regional focus: Allow users to select specific coastal regions for localized analysis.
  • Scenario comparison: Compare low, mid, and high emissions scenarios side by side.

Contributions

Suggestions for additional data sources, methodology improvements, or features are welcome. This project aims to evolve with the needs of coastal planners and communities.
