      Recommended Actions

        Risk Levels

        Coastal zones are classified by projected inundation, frequency of flooding, and exposure. Each level informs different planning and regulatory actions.

        Low Risk

        Elevated & Protected

        Elevation: above 3m from mean sea level

        Areas with natural elevation or existing defenses. Minor flood exposure during extreme events. Suitable for standard development with basic drainage considerations.

        → Monitor; maintain drainage; no special zoning required

        Medium Risk

        Moderate Exposure

        Elevation: 1-3m from mean sea level

        Periodic flooding possible; storm surge and high tides pose moderate risk. Requires elevated structures, flood-resistant design, and enhanced drainage systems.

        → Conditional zoning; elevation requirements; flood-proofing

        High Risk

        Critical Exposure

        Elevation: below 1m from mean sea level

        Frequent flooding; high vulnerability to storm surge and sea level rise. Not recommended for new residential development. Existing settlements need relocation or major fortification.

        → Restrict new development; prioritize evacuation planning

        Impacts on Infrastructure

        Critical coastal infrastructure faces increasing exposure to flooding, erosion, and saltwater intrusion as sea levels rise.

        🏗️

        Built Environment

        • Roads and bridges submerged or eroded
        • Ports and harbors require constant dredging
        • Water and wastewater systems at risk of inundation
        • Power substations and transmission lines exposed
        🏘️

        Coastal Settlements

        • Homes and businesses in low-lying areas flooded
        • Population displacement and migration pressure
        • Fisheries and tourism livelihoods disrupted
        • Schools, hospitals, and emergency services affected

        Zoning Recommendations

        Risk-based zoning guides where and how development occurs. Key principles include:

        📋

        Planning Actions

        • Update floodplain maps with sea level rise projections
        • Set elevation requirements for new construction
        • Designate buffer zones along coastlines
        • Integrate green infrastructure (wetlands, dunes)
        🛡️

        Protection Priorities

        • Protect critical infrastructure (hospitals, utilities)
        • Invest in nature-based and engineered defenses
        • Plan for managed retreat in highest-risk zones
        • Coordinate with evacuation and emergency plans
