Evacuation Planning

Preparing coastal communities for safe and timely response

As sea levels rise and storm surges intensify, evacuation planning becomes essential to protect human life. This section focuses on identifying vulnerable populations, prioritizing response strategies, and ensuring effective coordination during coastal emergencies.

High-Risk Population Zones

Certain regions and communities face heightened evacuation risks due to low elevation, population density, and limited infrastructure.

Coastal Settlements

Low-lying coastal settlements experience frequent flooding and erosion. Limited access to elevated shelters increases evacuation urgency during extreme events.

Urban Coastal Cities

Dense urban areas face complex evacuation challenges due to traffic congestion, high-rise buildings, and constrained evacuation routes.

Evacuation Priorities

Evacuation priorities are based on exposure level, population vulnerability, and available response time.

High Priority — Immediate Threat Zones

Areas below critical elevation thresholds where flooding is imminent. Immediate evacuation is required to prevent loss of life.

Medium Priority — Short-Term Impact Zones

Regions likely to be affected within hours or days, allowing phased evacuation and shelter preparation.

Low Priority — Preparedness Zones

Areas currently safe but at future risk, focusing on monitoring, awareness, and emergency preparedness.
